How to play

Two players, one hidden chain. Get from start to target by naming players who were teammates — each name must have shared a club, in the same seasons, with the player before him.

  • Par is the shortest chain that exists. You get par + 3 guesses — right or wrong, every guess costs one.
  • The career lines under your current player and the target are your map: find where two paths crossed.
  • Each player can appear in the chain only once — don't route yourself into a dead end.
  • NBA stints count too — some of the best routes go through the League. 😉

Game modes

  • Daily — one pair a day, two players in between, the same for everyone. Keep your streak alive.
  • Easy / Medium / Hard — one, two or three players stand between the pair.

Teammates are judged by the careers we store — the same well-travelled pool as The Grid — so a real-life teammate outside this data won't count.

How to play

A club's roster appears one name at a time — benchwarmers first, stars last. Name the club as early as you dare.

  • Reveal next shows another player, free — but the fewer names you need, the better the win.
  • You get 3 guesses. A wrong guess also forces the next name out.
  • Naming it from the first name or two is scout's-eye territory. 🤯

Game modes

  • Active — random 2025–26 rosters, as many as you like.
  • Legends — name the club from its retired greats instead.
  • Both — a mix: each round is either a current roster or a set of legends.

How to play

Fill all 9 cells. Tap a cell, then name any player who fits both its row and its column — e.g. played for Olympiacos and is French.

  • You get 12 guesses for the 9 cells — right or wrong, every guess costs one.
  • Each player can only be used once on the board, and most cells have several right answers.
  • A player counts for a club if it's anywhere in his career — including short stints and NBA spells.
  • Fill the board without a single miss for an Immaculate badge. 🤩

Game modes

  • Daily — one grid a day, the same board for everyone. Keep your streak alive.
  • Practice — random grids, as many as you like.

To keep answers fair, The Grid only uses well-travelled players and legends whose full career we know — so a newcomer still at his first club won't be accepted even if he'd fit.

How to play

You're shown a player and his clubs in a scrambled order. Put them back into the chronological order he played for them — earliest at the top, latest at the bottom.

Ordering

  • Drag a club by its ≡ handle to move it, or use the ▲ / ▼ buttons.
  • Hit Check order — clubs in the right spot lock green and stay green (until you move them). The years stay hidden until the end.
  • You get 3 checks; solve it before they run out or the answer is revealed.

Game modes

  • Daily — one career a day, the same for everyone. Keep your streak alive.
  • Easy / Medium / Hard — shorter to longer careers (more clubs = harder).

How to play

Guess the mystery EuroLeague player in 8 tries. Start typing a name and pick from the list. After each guess, every column is coloured to show how close you are.

Colours

  • ✓ Green — exact match
  • ~ Yellow — close (depends on the column)
  • · Grey — no match

What each column means

  • Team — same club, different club in the same country.
    e.g. you guess a Panathinaikos player and the answer plays for Olympiacos → both Greek → yellow.
  • Nat. — same nationality (where the player is from), otherwise grey.
  • Pos — same position (Guard / Forward / Center), otherwise grey.
  • Height — exact, within 5 cm.
  • Age — exact, within 2 years.
  • # — exact jersey number, within 3.

Arrows

On Height, Age and #, an ↑ means the answer's value is higher than your guess, and ↓ means it's lower.

How to play

Find the four groups of four hiding among the 16 names. Tap up to four tiles, then Submit. You get 3 mistakes before the puzzle reveals itself.

The four groups

Colours run easiest → hardest, and the themes change from puzzle to puzzle:

  • A club's current roster
  • A nationality — or all guards / forwards / centers
  • A Final Four five, everyone at one Final Four, or a birth decade
  • Club legends, ex-players of a club, a shared jersey number, the 2.10 m club, or journeymen

Each puzzle has exactly one group of each colour.

Tips

  • “One away…” means three of your four picks belong to the same group.
  • Watch for red herrings — a nationality, an old club or a jersey number can lure you into the wrong group.
  • Shuffle rearranges the tiles; Deselect all clears your picks.

Game modes

  • Daily — one puzzle a day, the same board for everyone. Keep your streak alive.
  • Practice — unlimited random puzzles.
